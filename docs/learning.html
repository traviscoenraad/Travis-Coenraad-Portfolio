My Learning Journey

Digital Horizon Week

Date: 28 October 2025 - 29 October 2025

Description: Digital Horizon Week was a university organised event for our professional development as computer science students. This program involved research that was being done by different computing research groups, attending employability sessions which taught us about placements and career planning. Then participating in industry-led talks and presentations. These sessions gave me exposure to both academic research and real-world industry practices within the technology sector.

Key Learnings: In the Digital Horizon Week, I got a better understanding of how academic research connects to the real-world technology development. The employability sessions gave me a lot of information about how placements work which was very important to me as I do intend on doing a placement. The industry presentations also showed the real-world impact of cyber security as in the Jaguar Land Rover Q&A they explained how Jaguar lost a lot of money due to a cyber attack and the importance of having cyber security professionals in companies.

Reflection: This experienced helped me to understand that cyber security is not just a technical concern but also a major business risk. For my career goal of becoming a Solutions Architect, this highlighted the importance of designing secure systems to make sure that technical decisions support both security and business objectives.

Before this event, I thought cyber security was very technical but now I see how security decisions affect business outcomes. Like how Jaguar Land Rover was hacked and lost a lot of money because of it.

NTU Stem Careers Fair

Date: 11 - 12 November 2025

Description: The career fair involved multiple industry stands where company representatives presented their organisations. They talked about internships, placements and graduate opportunities that were available. The event gave me exposure to multiple industries not just in the technology market but also finance and consulting.

Key Learnings: This event helped me understand the importance of transferable skills as well as technical knowledge. I learned that employers value communication, adaptability and problem-solving as much as academic qualifications. Something very important that I learnt was from a Law graduate who got a Finance role. He told me that a Computer Science background is highly respected and flexible across multiple industries and that if you are willing and adaptable you can transition into other industries.

Reflection: This experience helped me understand that technical ability alone will not help me succeed. For my goal of becoming a Solutions Architect, I will need strong communication and business awareness. The careers fair has helped me to understand that I need to develop my soft skills alongside my technical skills and be willing to adapt.

This event has helped me realise that career paths in technology are not linear and made me realise that adaptability and transferable skills are just as important as technical ability.

TryHackMe Pre-Security Learning Path (Partial Complete)

Date: 20 December 2025

Description: The TryHackMe Pre-Security learning path was an introductory cyber security programme. This programme helped me build foundational knowledge of how systems and networks work. I completed all available free modules. These modules gave a hands-on learning experience in key cyber security principles.

Key Learnings: Throughout this learning path, I developed a foundational understanding of networking concepts and common security risks. This course showed me the importance of understanding technologies and how they function. It also made me aware of how vulnerabilities can come from poor system design.

Reflection: This experience supports my goal of becoming a Solutions Architect by showing me the importance of secure systems. Even at foundation level, I now have a better understanding of potential risks when designing systems. This has encouraged me to continue to develop my skills in cyber alongside my software and system design skills to create more secure systems.

This learning path has made me aware that many security weakness come from poor system design. This has motivated me to think and plan my future designs for projects.

Telstra Cybersecurity Virutal Experience (Forage)

Date: 12 Janaury 2026

Description: The Telstra Cybersecurity Virtual Experience is an online job simulation designed to show the user real-world tasks that are done in a cybersecurity teams. I stepped into the role of a Telstra cybersecurity team member and completed guided tasks that helped me to develop understanding, communicating and responding to a distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attack.

Key Learnings: This experience taught me how cyber incidents are identified and analysed. This simulation demonstrated how technical teams must explain incidents and mitigation steps to non-technical stakeholders. It also showed me the important of cyber and the impact it has on businesses.

Reflection: This experience improved my understanding of how cybersecurity is used in business and decision making. It has shown me the importance of designing secure systems which supports my goal of becoming a Solutions Architect. I now understand that security is not only a technical issue but a business and communication challenge too.

This made me realise my limited experience in communicating technical incidents. This made me aware of an area I urgently need to develop if I want to work in roles that involve decision-making and stakeholder interactions.

Reflection on Future Development

Date: 2026

Overall Reflection: Overall, these activities have helped me realise that becoming a Solutions Architect requires more than just technical skill. While I have only developed foundational skills, my current experience is mainly academic. My priority is to gain experience in real-world systems through placements and internships, while also continuing my development of understanding how to design secure systems. These activities have helped me to think about how and when I can apply these skills.

Of all the CPD activities I have done, the Telstra Cybersecurity Virtual Experience had the greatest impact on my development. It made me realise how much I need to learn to work in a cyber security role within a company. It combined technical ability with communication which are essential to succeed in cyber roles.

Future Development: I plan to continue developing both my technical and professional skills. I want to strengthen my understanding of system architecture, cloud platforms and how to design secure applications. These goals support my long-term career goal of becoming a Solutions Architect. I want to build more projects that integrate software and security development. For professional development, I will focus on my communication skills especially when it comes to explaining technical concepts to non-technical people. I plan to gain industry exposure through placements, internships and job simulations. By continuing this alongside my studies, I believe I can become a well-rounded graduate capable of designing secure and scalable business solutions.
