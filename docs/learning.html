My Learning Journey

Digital Horizon Week

Date: 28 October 2025 - 29 October 2025

Description: Digital Horizon Week was a university organised event for our professional development as computer science students. This program involved research that was being done by different computing research groups, attending employability sessions which taught us about placements and career planning. Then participating in industry-led talks and presentations. These sessions gave me exposure to both academic research and real-world industry practices within the technology sector.

Key Learnings: In the Digital Horizon Week, I got a better understanding of how academic research connects to the real-world technology development. The employability sessions gave me a lot of information about how placements work which was very important to me as I do intend on doing a placement. The industry presentations also showed the real-world impact of cyber security as in the Jaguar Land Rover Q&A they explained how Jaguar lost a lot of money due to a cyber attack and the importance of having cyber security professionals in companies.

Reflection: This experienced helped me to understand that cyber security is not just a technical concern but also a major business risk. For my career goal of becoming a Solutions Architect, this highlighted the important of designing secure systems to make sure that technical decisions support both security and business objectives.

NTU Stem Careers Fair

Date: 11 - 12 Novemeber 2025

Description: The career fair involved multiple industry stands where company representatives presented their organisations. They talked about internships, placements and graduate opportunities that were available. The event gave me exposure to multiple industries not just in the technology market but also finance and consulting.

Key Learnings: This event helped me understand the importance of transferable skills as well as technical knowledge. I learned that employers value communication, adaptability and problem-solving as much as academic qualifications. Something very important that I learnt was from a graduate who went from Law into Finance and told me that Computer Science background is highly respected and flexible across multiple industries.

Reflection: This experience helped me understand that technical ability alone will not help me succeed. For my goal of becoming a Solutions Architect, I will need strong communication and business awareness. The careers fair has helped me to understand that I need to develop my soft skills alongside my technical skills and be willing to adapt.
